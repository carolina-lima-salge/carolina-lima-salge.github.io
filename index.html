Welcome

I am an Assistant Professor in the School of Business at Wake Forest University.

I received my PhD from the MIS Department at Terry College of Business, University of Georgia. My dissertation focuses on understanding the use of bots in online social networks, and the ethics surrounding their use. Bots are computer algorithms sharing messages, uploading pictures, and connecting with many users on places like Twitter and Facebook and they can be used in a variety of ways, as for example, to explore the dark web and to disseminate live news about earthquakes. I obtained my master's and bachelor's degrees in Language & International Trade (Spanish) and Economics at Clemson University.

My current research aims at generating insights to improve social interactions with algorithms in ethical ways. Specifically, I am interested in (1) using case studies to illustrate how bots disseminate information in online social networks; (2) developing theory to explain the value of conversational agents in an increasingly hyper-private web environment; (3) clarifying the complexities associated with computer algorithmic transparency, and theorizing about its potential benefits for managing AI.
